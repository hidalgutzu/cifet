PROFESSIONAL INTERNSHIPS IN HOSPITALITY AND TOURISM                


 


Tourism internships are designed to develop students’ skills through performing the actual tasks involved in a pre-determined position at an upscale hotel.

Internships last from 3 to 6 months, 8 hours a day, 6 days a week.

Practice begins with an induction period to familiarize the students with characteristics, standards and operating policies of the hotel and then gradually progressing to actual duties, which are performed under the supervision of hotel executives. Upon completion of the program, a  hotel will  maks an assessment of each trainee´s performance and award a program completion certificate/diploma.

You can choose to intern in the following areas:


• Reception
• Housekeeping (floor supervisor)
• Public Relations
• Organization of groups and conventions
• Food and beverage
• Kitchen
• Administration

Requirements:

• Commitment to exceptional service
• Basic proficiency in English (except for kitchen internships)
• Accident and health insurance valid in Cancun, Mexico
• “Student Visa”.  The first step is to apply to a CIFET program, which will then arrange an “entry permit” with the National Immigration Institute of Mexico on your behalf. This permit wil thenl be sent to the student to be presented at the Consulate of Mexico in his/her country of origin.


Calendar

• Internships can start at any time of year
• Application must be submitted three months prior to the scheduled start date

Your stay in Cancun:

“CIFET” will provide the following to internship program participants:

• Work permit from the immigration authority (for internship purpose only)
• Pick up at the airport and transportation to the residence
• Introductory presentation to the internship program
• Supervision and support throughout the internship
• Final evaluation and certificate
• Uniforms (loaned for the duration of the program)
• Student accommodation
• Weekly change of linens and room cleaning
• One meal per day
